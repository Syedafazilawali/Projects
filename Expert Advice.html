Understanding good nutrition and paying attention to what you eat can help you maintain or improve your health

Psychology and Behaviour


What is Behavioral Psychology?

Let's define behavioral psychology. Behavioral psychology is the study of the connection between our minds and our behavior. Sometimes you will hear behavioral psychology referred to as behaviorism. The researchers and scientists who study behavioral psychology are trying to understand why we behave the way we do and they are concerned with discovering patterns in our actions and behaviors. The hope is that if we can use behavioral psychology to help us predict how humans will behave, we can build better habits as individuals, create better products as companies, and develop better living spaces as communities.

Health and Medicine


Distinctions Between Public Health and Medicine


Public Health:-


1.Primary focus on populations
2.Public service ethic, as an extension of concerns for the individual
3.Emphasis on disease prevention and health promotion for the whole community
4.Public health paradigm employs a spectrum of interventions aimed at the environment, human behavior and lifestyle,
5.and medical care
6.Variable certification of specialists beyond professional public health degree
7.Lines of specialization organized, for example, by: analytical method (epidemiology, toxicology)
8.setting and population (occupational health, global health)
9.substantive health problem (environmental health, nutrition)
10.Life sciences central, with a prime focus on major threats to the health of populations; research moves between laboratory and field
11.Population sciences and quantitative disciplines essential features of analysis and training
12.Social and public policy disciplines an integral part of public health education

Medicine:-

1.Primary focus on individual
2.Personal service ethic, in the context of social responsibilities
3.Emphasis on disease diagnosis, treatment, and care for the individual patient
4.Medical paradigm places predominant emphasis on medical care
5.Uniform system for certifying specialists beyond professional medical degree
6.Lines of specialization organized, for example, by:
7.organ system (cardiology, neurology)
8.patient group (obstetrics, pediatrics)
9.etiology and pathophysiology (infectious disease, oncology)
10.technical skill (radiology, surgery)
11.Biological sciences central, stimulated by needs of patients; research moves between laboratory and bedside
12.Numerical sciences increasing in prominence, though still a relatively minor part of training
13.Social sciences tend to be an elective part of medical education

Exercise


What are the health benefits of exercise?



Exercise is not just about aerobic capacity and muscle size. Sure, exercise can improve your physical health and your physique, trim your waistline, improve your sex life, and even add years to your life. But that’s not what motivates most people to stay active.

People who exercise regularly tend to do so because it gives them an enormous sense of well-being. They feel more energetic throughout the day, sleep better at night, have sharper memories, and feel more relaxed and positive about themselves and their lives. And it’s also powerful medicine for many common mental health challenges.

Regular exercise can have a profoundly positive impact on depression, anxiety, ADHD, and more. It also relieves stress, improves memory, helps you sleep better, and boosts your overall mood. And you don’t have to be a fitness fanatic to reap the benefits. Research indicates that modest amounts of exercise can make a difference. No matter your age or fitness level, you can learn to use exercise as a powerful tool to feel better.

Stress and Mental Well-Being


Are you experiencing signs of stress?


Stress can affect your body, your behaviours, your feelings and your thoughts. Here are common signs of stress:
Changes in your body
1.Tense muscles
2.Rapid breathing and heart rate
3.Headaches
4.Difficulties sleeping well
5.Fatigue
6.Changes in sex drive
7.Weaker immune system
8.Changes in your behaviours
9.Withdrawing from others
10.Fidgeting, feeling restless
11.Smoking, drinking or using more drugs than usual
12.Avoiding situations that you think are stressful
13.Changes in your feelings
14.Feeling worried or confused
15.Feeling angry or irritable
16.Feeling overwhelmed or helpless
17.Feeling like you can't cope
18.Changes in your thoughts
19.Struggling to concentrate, remember or make decisions
20.Losing your self-confidence
21.Having a negative attitude towards yourself and your life
